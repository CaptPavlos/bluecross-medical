Understanding the Two Regulatory Frameworks

Medical devices used in maritime settings can fall under two completely different regulatory systems. The critical distinction determines Cardisiography's classification.

‚ùå MED Directive (Maritime Equipment)

Applies To:

  • Life-saving appliances (lifeboats, rafts, AEDs)
  • Navigation equipment (ECDIS, radar)
  • Fire protection systems
  • Radio/communication equipment
  • Propulsion & machinery systems

Does NOT Apply To:

  • ‚ùå Diagnostic medical devices (ECG, Cardisiography)
  • ‚ùå Medical examination equipment
  • ‚ùå Ship's medicine chests

‚úÖ EU-MDR (Medical Device Regulation)

Applies To:

  • Electrocardiographs (ECG machines)
  • Cardisiography systems
  • Blood pressure monitors
  • Diagnostic imaging equipment
  • Patient monitors

Governing Authority:

EU Notified Body conformity assessment

Why Cardisiography Falls Under EU-MDR, NOT MED

The MED Directive only applies to equipment installed on ships that serve maritime safety functions (navigation, fire protection, life-saving, emergency response).

Cardisiography is used for medical diagnosis and screening, which is a healthcare function, not a maritime safety function.

Therefore, it falls exclusively under medical device regulations, not maritime equipment rules.
